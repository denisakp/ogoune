## TL;DR

You may:

- ✅ **Fork the code** under Apache 2.0 and redistribute it (that's the OSS spirit)
- ✅ Say your project is **"based on Ogoune"** or **"compatible with Ogoune"**
- ✅ Use the Ogoune logo in screenshots, articles, conferences, comparison sites
- ✅ Build third-party integrations (`X for Ogoune`) when they are genuinely compatible

You may not, without written permission:

- ❌ Rename a fork **"Ogoune Pro"**, **"Ogoune Plus"**, **"Ogoune Enhanced"**, etc.
- ❌ Sell a service using the name **Ogoune** or a confusingly similar derivative
- ❌ Use the Ogoune logo on your product as if it were official
- ❌ Register a domain like `ogoune-*.com` for commercial purposes
- ❌ Present your fork as **the** official version of Ogoune

## Forks and redistributions

Apache 2.0 grants you the right to **fork, modify, and redistribute** Ogoune's code. This policy does not restrict that. But the name "Ogoune" itself is not covered by Apache 2.0 — it is separately protected by trademark law.

### If you fork and redistribute

Rename your fork. Acceptable examples:

- ✅ "MonitorX (based on Ogoune)"
- ✅ "AcmeWatch — Ogoune-compatible"
- ✅ "FastUptime, originally derived from Ogoune"

Refused examples:

- ❌ "Ogoune Enhanced"
- ❌ "Ogoune Community Plus"
- ❌ "OgouneX"

The code stays under Apache 2.0. You publish whatever you want. But under a different name.

### Minor modifications

If you apply minor patches (bug fixes, security) without changing the nature of the product, you may distribute it internally (your company) under the name "Ogoune" with patch mention. For public distribution of a patched binary, rename.

## Editorial use

### Articles, blogs, books, conferences

You may freely refer to Ogoune by name and use its logo in editorial contexts. No permission required.

### Comparisons

Comparison sites ("Ogoune vs Better Uptime"), tables, infographics: allowed without permission, provided the comparisons are factual.

### Tutorials and training

Creating a tutorial, training course, YouTube video using Ogoune: allowed. You may use the logo and the name. You **may not** present yourself as official Ogoune training without agreement.

## Integrations and plugins

If you build a third-party integration that genuinely interfaces with Ogoune (notification channel, exporter, dashboard, etc.):

- ✅ "X for Ogoune": allowed (e.g., "Datadog Exporter for Ogoune")
- ✅ "Connect to Ogoune": allowed
- ✅ Using the Ogoune logo in your plugin's documentation: allowed

Required attribution: "Ogoune is a trademark of Denis Akpagnonite, used with permission."

## Commercial hosting

If you offer a commercial service hosting Ogoune Community Edition for your customers:

- ✅ You may do so (Apache 2.0 permits commercial use)
- ✅ You may present it as "managed Ogoune hosting"
- ❌ You may not use **"Ogoune Cloud"** or **"Ogoune Hosted"** — these names are reserved for Ogoune's official commercial offering
- Rename: e.g., "AcmeHost Managed Monitoring (powered by Ogoune)"

---

## Non-commercial forks

For personal, experimental, or educational forks without intent of commercial redistribution: this policy applies in the literal sense, but we do not pursue non-commercial uses in good faith.
